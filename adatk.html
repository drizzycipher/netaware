Digital literacy gives more than device skills. It teaches safe habits for searching, evaluating, and sharing information online.

In daily life people use smartphones for communication, shopping, and banking. That convenience increases risk. This guide lists concrete habits to lower that risk while keeping tasks fast and simple.

Practical steps

  • Use unique passwords and a password manager.
  • Enable two factor authentication where available.
  • Keep devices and apps updated.
  • Avoid unknown links or downloads.
  • Do not act on random antivirus popups.
  • Back up critical files regularly.
  • Limit what you share on social media.
  • Use a VPN on public Wi Fi.

1. Strong passwords: Use long, unique passphrases rather than single words.

2. Two factor: Use app based codes rather than SMS when possible.

3. Updates: Install OS and app updates to patch vulnerabilities.

4. Backups: Keep a copy of important files in cloud storage or an external drive.

⚠️ Beware of fake antivirus alerts. Real security tools do not appear as random browser popups. Close the tab. Do not click “Fix” or “Download.”
